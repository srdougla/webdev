Welcome to My Virtual Pet!

Visit the animal shelter to adopt your first pet.

Pets bring joy, comfort, and companionship to millions of people around the world. From playful puppies and curious cats to silly ferrets and gentle turtles, pets come in all shapes and sizes, but they all share a special bond with their owners. Studies show that spending time with pets can reduce stress, lower blood pressure, and even boost happiness by increasing levels of oxytocin, the "feel-good” hormone. Pets also teach responsibility and empathy, as caring for another living being requires patience, routine, and love. Whether they’re wagging their tails, purring softly, or simply sitting by your side, pets have an amazing way of making a house feel like home.

Pet Habitat & Environment Requirements

Pet Preferred Temperature Humidity Space / Habitat Lighting Special Notes
Cat 65–80°F (18–27°C) Normal indoor levels Indoor living, litter box, climbing spaces Natural sunlight or indoor lighting Provide vertical spaces and hiding spots for enrichment
Turtle 75–85°F (24–29°C) basking; water 72–78°F (22–26°C) 50–70% (higher for tropical species) Aquarium or terrarium with land and water areas UVB light for 10–12 hours/day Provide basking platforms; avoid sudden temperature drops
Ferret 60–75°F (16–24°C) Normal indoor levels Large cage with tunnels, bedding, and play areas Natural or indoor lighting Ferrets love exploring; supervised floor play is recommended
Snake 70–90°F (21–32°C), depending on species 40–70% (species dependent) Secure terrarium with hide boxes UVB optional (depends on species) Provide heat gradients; avoid drafts and escapes
